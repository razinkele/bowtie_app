# Offshore Wind Farm Biodiversity & Fishery Impact Scenario

## Overview

This document describes the new BowTie scenario template for assessing offshore wind farm impacts on marine biodiversity and fishery, with integrated Nature Inclusive Design (NID) controls.

**Version**: 5.4.0
**Date**: January 2026
**Scenario ID**: `offshore_wind_biodiversity`

## Scenario Description

### Central Problem
**Offshore wind farm impacts on marine biodiversity and fishery resources**

### Project Scope
- **Location**: North Sea / Baltic Sea / Mediterranean Sea - Offshore Wind Farm Zone
- **Project Type**: Marine
- **Scale**: Regional
- **Urgency**: High

### Detailed Problem Statement
Assessment of multi-phase impacts from offshore wind energy development including:

1. **Construction Phase**
   - Pile driving noise
   - Seabed disturbance
   - Sediment suspension
   - Vessel traffic

2. **Operation Phase**
   - Electromagnetic fields from cables
   - Operational noise
   - Habitat modification
   - Fishing exclusion zones
   - Collision risk for birds/bats

3. **Decommissioning Phase**
   - Structure removal
   - Habitat loss
   - Sediment disturbance

## BowTie Structure

### Causes (Activities)
From existing vocabulary (CAUSES.xlsx):
- **A3.1**: Renewable energy generation (wind, wave and tidal power)
- **A3.3**: Transmission of electricity and communications (cables)
- **A1.4**: Offshore structures (other than for oil/gas/renewables)
- **A1.5**: Restructuring of seabed morphology

### Pressures
From existing vocabulary:
- **P2.1**: Physical disturbance to seabed (temporary or reversible)
- **P2.2**: Physical loss (due to permanent change of seabed substrate)
- **P2.3**: Changes to hydrological conditions
- **P2.4**: New habitat creation
- **P3.5**: Input of anthropogenic sound (impulsive, continuous)
- **P3.6**: Input of other forms of energy (including electromagnetic fields)
- **P1.5**: Disturbance of species (where they breed, rest and feed)
- **P1.6**: Extraction of, or mortality/injury to, wild species

### Consequences
From existing vocabulary (CONSEQUENCES.xlsx):
- **C1.1**: Change in ecosystem/marine processes
- **C1.2.1**: Decrease in provision of fish & shellfish
- **C2.2**: Decreased income (fishery sector)
- **C2.5**: Negative impact on Blue Economy development
- **C3.1**: Decrease of aesthetic value/benefits

## Usage Instructions

### Method 1: Environmental Scenario Templates (Quick Start)
1. Navigate to **Data Upload** tab
2. In **Environmental Scenario** dropdown, select:
   - "Offshore wind farm impacts on marine biodiversity and fishery"
3. Click **Generate Sample Data**
4. The system will generate a focused bowtie with:
   - Central Problem: "Offshore wind farm impacts on marine biodiversity and fishery"
   - Activities: Pile driving, Cable laying, Turbine operation, Vessel traffic
   - Pressures: Underwater noise, Seabed disturbance, EMF exposure, Fishing displacement
   - Preventive Controls: Bubble curtains, Soft-start, Radar monitoring (NID)
   - Consequences: Marine mammal displacement, Fish decline, Habitat loss
   - Mitigating Controls: Artificial reefs, Biohuts, Oyster restoration (NID)

### Method 2: Guided Workflow (Detailed)
1. Navigate to **Guided Workflow** tab
2. In **Step 1 (Project Setup)**, select the environmental scenario:
   - "Offshore wind farm impacts on marine biodiversity and fishery"
3. The template will auto-fill:
   - Project name and location
   - Central problem statement
   - Problem details and category
4. Continue through steps to select:
   - Activities (Step 3): Select from A3.x renewable energy activities
   - Pressures (Step 3): Select noise, EMF, seabed disturbance pressures
   - Preventive Controls (Step 4): Select NID controls Ctrl2.3.1-2.3.8
   - Consequences (Step 5): Select biodiversity and fishery impacts
   - Mitigating Controls (Step 6): Select NID controls Ctrl2.3.9-2.3.27

### For Direct Data Entry
Use the vocabulary selectors to find:
- **Controls**: Search for "Nature Inclusive" or "NID" or specific techniques like "Biohut", "reef", "bubble curtain"
